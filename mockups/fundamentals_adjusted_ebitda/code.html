1D. Adjusted EBITDA

In the Lower Middle Market (LMM), "Adjusted EBITDA" is the true gold standard. While standard EBITDA gives a rough proxy for cash flow, it often includes non-recurring, unusual, or personal expenses that wouldn't persist under a Private Equity owner.

Why it's used in LMM:

  • arrow_forward Owner Personal Expenses: Normalizing "lifestyle" costs like personal car leases or non-business travel.
  • arrow_forward One-time Legal Fees: Stripping out extraordinary costs that won't happen again next year.
  • arrow_forward Market Salary: Adjusting the owner's salary to what a professional CEO would cost.
